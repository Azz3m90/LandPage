FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about FastCaisse Restaurant POS

What makes FastCaisse the #1 certified POS in Belgium?

FastCaisse is officially registered and certified by the Belgian Federal Public Service Finance (FPS Finance). Our system meets all legal requirements for cash registers in Belgium, including secure data storage and tamper-proof transaction logging. This certification ensures complete compliance with Belgian fiscal regulations, protecting your business from legal issues.

How does the Kitchen Display System work?

Our Kitchen Display System (KDS) provides real-time communication between the front-of-house and kitchen. When an order is placed, it instantly appears on screens in the kitchen and bar areas with clear visual organization by station. The system supports order timing, order modification alerts, and can be configured for different preparation stages, ensuring efficient order fulfillment and reducing errors.

What are the benefits of the Self-Service Kiosk Module?

The Self-Service Kiosk Module allows customers to place orders independently at their convenience. This reduces wait times, decreases the workload on staff, and can lead to increased average order value as customers can browse the menu at their own pace. Kiosks operate 24/7, increasing potential sales, and help optimize staff allocation to other critical tasks. They also improve order accuracy as customers enter their selections directly.

Can I integrate with delivery platforms like UberEats and Takeaway.com?

Yes, FastCaisse offers specific integration modules for major delivery platforms including UberEats and Takeaway.com. This allows orders from these platforms to flow directly into your FastCaisse system, streamlining order management and reducing the chance of errors from manual entry. You can manage all orders, whether dine-in, takeaway, or delivery, from a single interface.

Can I have my own e-commerce website?

Yes, FastCaisse offers the ability to provide you with your own dedicated e-commerce website. This functions similarly to popular platforms like Takeaway.com or UberEats but is branded for your restaurant. It allows customers to order directly from you, giving you more control over the customer experience and keeping delivery fees in-house, potentially increasing your profit margins.
